Lesson 4: Social Media Phishing - How Attackers Use Facebook, Instagram, and Twitter

What is Social Media Phishing?

Social media phishing is when attackers use platforms like Facebook, Instagram, or Twitter to trick users into revealing personal information, clicking malicious links, or downloading malware. These attacks often appear as direct messages, fake friend requests, or posts that look like they're from trusted brands or friends.

  • Fake Login Pages: Attackers create lookalike login pages to steal your username and password.
  • Impersonation: Scammers pretend to be your friends, celebrities, or brands to gain your trust.
  • Malicious Links: Links in posts or messages may lead to phishing sites or malware downloads.
  • Prize Scams: "You've won!" messages ask for your info or get you to click a dangerous link.
  • Account Recovery Scams: Attackers claim your account is at risk and ask for your credentials to "help" you.

Example: You get a DM on Instagram from a friend saying, "Check out this video of you!" with a link. The link leads to a fake Instagram login page designed to steal your password.

How to Spot Social Media Phishing

  • Check the sender's profile for oddities (few followers, no posts, strange username).
  • Be wary of urgent messages or those asking for personal info.
  • Hover over links (on desktop) to see where they really go.
  • Look for spelling errors or awkward language.
  • Never enter your password on a page you reached via a suspicious link.

How to Protect Yourself

  • Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) on all social accounts.
  • Don't share passwords or sensitive info via DMs.
  • Report suspicious accounts and messages to the platform.
  • Keep your apps and devices updated.
  • Educate friends and family about social media scams.

Remember: If something feels off, it probably is. Always double-check before clicking or sharing information!
